# 🧠 Biological Neuron

## 📖 Introduction

A **Biological Neuron** is the basic structural and functional unit of the human nervous system. It is a specialized cell responsible for receiving, processing, and transmitting information through electrical and chemical signals.

The human brain contains approximately **86 billion neurons** that work together to perform activities such as thinking, learning, remembering, speaking, seeing, hearing, and controlling body movements.

Scientists studied the working of biological neurons and used them as inspiration to develop **Artificial Neurons**, which are the building blocks of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Deep Learning.

---

# 🧩 Parts of a Biological Neuron

A biological neuron mainly consists of four important parts.

## 1️⃣ Dendrites

Dendrites are tree-like branches that receive signals from other neurons or sensory organs.

### Function
- Receive incoming signals.
- Transfer signals to the cell body.

Example:
When you touch a hot object, dendrites receive the pain signal.

---

## 2️⃣ Cell Body (Soma)

The Cell Body is the central part of the neuron.

It contains the nucleus and controls all activities of the neuron.

### Function
- Processes incoming information.
- Maintains the health of the neuron.
- Decides whether the signal should continue.

---

## 3️⃣ Axon

The Axon is a long fiber that carries electrical signals away from the cell body.

### Function
- Transmits electrical impulses.
- Sends information to the next neuron.

Some axons can be more than one meter long.

---

## 4️⃣ Synapse

The Synapse is the small gap between two neurons.

Neurons do not physically touch each other.

Chemical messengers called **Neurotransmitters** carry the signal across this gap.

### Function
- Transfers signals from one neuron to another.
- Enables communication inside the nervous system.

---

# ⚡ Working of a Biological Neuron

The working process follows these steps:

1. Dendrites receive information.
2. The Cell Body processes the information.
3. The Axon carries the electrical impulse.
4. The Synapse transfers the signal to the next neuron.

This process happens continuously inside the human brain.

- Dendrites
- Cell Body (Soma)

- Synapse

# 📚 Summary

A biological neuron is the fundamental unit of the human nervous system. It receives information through dendrites, processes it in the cell body, transmits it through the axon, and communicates with other neurons using synapses. This natural communication system inspired the creation of artificial neurons, which form the foundation of modern Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning.
