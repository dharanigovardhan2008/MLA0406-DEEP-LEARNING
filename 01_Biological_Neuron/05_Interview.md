# 🎤 Biological Neuron - Interview Questions

## Q1. What is a Biological Neuron?

A Biological Neuron is the basic structural and functional unit of the nervous system. It receives, processes, and transmits information through electrical and chemical signals.

---

## Q2. Why do we study Biological Neurons in Deep Learning?

Scientists designed Artificial Neurons by taking inspiration from Biological Neurons. Understanding biological neurons helps us understand how Artificial Neural Networks and Deep Learning models work.

---

## Q3. Name the four main parts of a Biological Neuron.

- Dendrites
- Cell Body (Soma)
- Axon
- Synapse

---

## Q4. What is the function of Dendrites?

Dendrites receive signals from other neurons and send them to the cell body.

---

## Q5. What is the function of the Cell Body (Soma)?

The cell body processes incoming signals, contains the nucleus, and controls the activities of the neuron.

---

## Q6. What is the function of the Axon?

The axon carries electrical impulses away from the cell body to other neurons or muscles.

---

## Q7. What is a Synapse?

A Synapse is the small gap between two neurons where neurotransmitters transfer signals.

---

## Q8. How does a Biological Neuron transmit information?

Information flows in the following order:

Dendrites → Cell Body → Axon → Synapse → Next Neuron

---

## Q9. What inspired the development of Artificial Neurons?

The structure and working of Biological Neurons inspired scientists to develop Artificial Neurons used in Artificial Intelligence.

---

## Q10. What is the difference between a Biological Neuron and an Artificial Neuron?

| Biological Neuron | Artificial Neuron |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Natural cell | Mathematical model |
| Uses electrical & chemical signals | Uses mathematical calculations |
| Found in the human brain | Found in computers |
| Learns through biological processes | Learns from data using algorithms |

---

# ⭐ Interview Tips

- Explain concepts in simple language.
- Mention the signal flow: Dendrites → Cell Body → Axon → Synapse.
- Relate Biological Neurons to Artificial Neurons.
- Draw a simple neuron diagram if asked in a technical interview or viva.
